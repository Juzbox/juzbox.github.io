## Parallel research tracks

The work resulted in the shadowing of the development of the [KultVis](https://smartbuilt.se/in-english/projects/innovations-and-new-applications/visualization-of-cultural-values-in-urban-planning-in-a-digital-twin/) digital twin and the production of my own digital twin prototypes inspired by the practice of counter-cartography. 

These two approaches to twinning revolved around two key sites, respectively, the [Gothenburg Film Studios](https://www.gothenburgfilmstudios.com/) and the [Gazaplatsen](https://www.instagram.com/gustudentsforpalestine/?hl=en)  encampment. The former was chosen prior to my joining the project as a site where economic development and cultural production cohabit fruitfully. The latter, a protest site, was chosen to explore how a city's digital twin might engage with efforts of civic contestation in a way that reflects the interests of its residents.

An in-depth account of the research [can be found here (📑)](https://gupea.ub.gu.se/items/07921d88-13fc-46f2-8ca3-df36e2742c78).

## Outcome: Counter-twinning
The outcome of this exploration is the development of "counter-twinning" a method inspired by counter-cartography and the discipline of [Forensic Architecture](https://forensic-architecture.org/) pioneered by the eponymous research agency. a method that subverts existing digital mapping and 3D modeling platforms to document marginalized spatial narratives and acts of collective resistance. Rather than adding features to existing urban digital twins, counter-twinning advocates for civic actors to appropriate and reconfigure the technical tools that constitute Gothenburg's digital ecosystem to gain negotiation power and visibility. 

This work culminated in the production of three counter-twinning prototypes and an open repository or wiki ([Virtual Gazaplatsen Lab](https://tinyurl.com/GPmapping)), offering a foundation for reimagining digital twins as public assets for collective liberation.
