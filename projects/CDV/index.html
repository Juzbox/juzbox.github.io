LA CITÉ DE L’IMAGE ET DU VIVANT

An online 3D space and strategy proposal for the programming of the CITÉ DE L’IMAGE ET DU VIVANT.

    How must image-making crafts and their education be reimagined in light of recently emerging digital possibilities?

    Overview

    BRIEF APPROACH
    Create guidelines and case studies to inform the programming of a cutting-edge campus dedicated to digital image production, in Arles, France.

    		 For this project I worked with Fabbula on two key deliverables:
    - The outlining of the educational programs to be offered on this campus which sought to embrace the increasingly multi-disciplinary nature of digital image making.
    - The production of a virtual exhibit in which visitors could see how the program was designed and attend virtual brainstorming
    sessions.

    Program design

    The vision was developed to reflect the increasingly porous nature of creative image-making through digital mediums. The program was informed by the close study of complementary sites, their educational offerings, and comparing them to Arles' specific context as a thriving cultural hub.

    Virtual spatial curation

    ❮ ❯

    ❮ ❯
    		The program was presented as a virtual space created in spatial.io which exhibited
    both the background research and the proposed vision.

    The online collaborative platform was chosen to welcome further co-creation efforts should the project be selected. At the end of the exhibit, visitors were invited to contribute their own needs, desires, and hopes, for what to include in the program.

    The virtual space was created using photogrammetric (3D scan) captures taken on site, GIS data, and a skybox taken in Google Stret View to situate the virtual space in the location the site will one day occupy.

    Role: UX Designer, Design Research

    Client: Redman, Fabbula

    Collaborators: Fabien Siouffi

    Location : Arles, France
